Pomodoro Technique

What it is: A time management method that uses 25-minute focused work sessions followed by 5-minute breaks.

Step-by-Step:

  1. Choose a task to work on
  2. Set timer for 25 minutes
  3. Work with full focus until timer rings
  4. Take a 5-minute break
  5. After 4 pomodoros, take a 15-30 minute break

Interval Templates:

  • Classic: 25 work / 5 break / 15 long break
  • Extended: 50 work / 10 break / 30 long break
  • Quick: 15 work / 3 break / 10 long break

Benefits:

  • Reduces mental fatigue
  • Improves focus and concentration
  • Creates urgency to complete tasks

Common Mistakes:

  • Skipping breaks (leads to burnout)
  • Checking phone during work sessions
  • Not planning tasks before starting

Blurting Method

What it is: A recall technique where you write/speak everything you remember about a topic, then check against your notes.

Step-by-Step:

  1. Read through your notes on a topic
  2. Close your notes
  3. Write/speak everything you remember
  4. Compare with original notes
  5. Identify and fill knowledge gaps

Sample Correct Answer Format:

Topic: Photosynthesis

Key points to remember: chloroplasts, light-dependent reactions, Calvin cycle, glucose production, CO2 + H2O ‚Üí C6H12O6 + O2

When to Use:

  • Before exams to test knowledge
  • After learning new material
  • When reviewing old topics

Active Recall

What it is: Testing yourself on material rather than passively re-reading it.

Step-by-Step:

  1. Read and understand the material
  2. Close your notes/book
  3. Try to recall key concepts from memory
  4. Check your answers
  5. Focus on what you got wrong

Flashcard Examples:

Front: What is the mitochondria?

Back: The powerhouse of the cell; produces ATP through cellular respiration

Benefits:

  • 3x more effective than re-reading
  • Strengthens memory pathways
  • Identifies knowledge gaps quickly

Spaced Repetition

What it is: Reviewing material at increasing intervals to move it into long-term memory.

Scheduling Template:

  • Day 1: Learn new material
  • Day 2: First review
  • Day 4: Second review
  • Day 7: Third review
  • Day 14: Fourth review
  • Day 30: Fifth review

When to Use:

  • Learning vocabulary
  • Memorizing facts/formulas
  • Long-term exam preparation

Feynman Technique

What it is: Explaining a concept in simple terms as if teaching a child.

Step-by-Step:

  1. Choose a concept
  2. Explain it in simple language
  3. Identify gaps in your explanation
  4. Review and simplify further

Simplification Example:

Complex: "Photosynthesis is the process by which photoautotrophs convert electromagnetic radiation into chemical energy."

Simple: "Plants use sunlight to make their own food, like having a built-in kitchen powered by the sun."

Interleaving

What it is: Mixing different topics or types of problems during study sessions.

Topic-Mixing Example:

Instead of: 30 min algebra, 30 min algebra, 30 min algebra

Do: 30 min algebra, 30 min geometry, 30 min statistics

Benefits:

  • Improves problem discrimination
  • Enhances long-term retention
  • Builds flexible thinking

PQ4R Method

What it is: Preview, Question, Read, Reflect, Recite, Review

Step-by-Step:

  1. Preview: Skim headings and summaries
  2. Question: Turn headings into questions
  3. Read: Read actively to answer questions
  4. Reflect: Think about what you read
  5. Recite: Summarize in your own words
  6. Review: Go over material again

Elaborative Interrogation

What it is: Asking "why" and "how" questions about facts you're learning.

Example:

Fact: Plants are green.

Why? Because chlorophyll reflects green light.

Why does chlorophyll reflect green? Its molecular structure absorbs red and blue wavelengths for photosynthesis.

Retrospective Timetabling

What it is: Recording what you actually studied rather than planning ahead.

How it works:

  1. After each study session, log what you studied
  2. Note the duration and topics covered
  3. Review weekly to see patterns
  4. Identify neglected subjects

Benefits:

  • Reduces planning anxiety
  • Shows real study patterns
  • Flexible and stress-free
